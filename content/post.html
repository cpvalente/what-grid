Circular

Circular is a project for a small format indoor lamp machined from a single block of wood. A simple, naked optics system built into a wooden circle.

Birch Alluminium Acrylic Electronics

Overview

There are three wooden parts to the project, a base, a mirror assembly and a circle where everything connects. Additionaly wooden details provide enclosers for lamp and dimmer. All parts are machines from wood. For contrast, some of the parts could be stained or treated similar to the render.

Assembly elements, from left to right:
Mirror, dimmer, lamp, LED engine, base

Lamp and base

The lamp main frame (left image) is CNCed from a single piece of wood. At the front there is a machined inset for the PCB insert (bottom left). The holes for the light (bottom left) and mirror assembly (top right) have to be done separetely. Potentially it would be possible to machine two parts jig for this operation out of the same wood block.

Light - Oslon LED Engine

Oslon LED engine as light source

The light source is a OSLONeye LED kit fitted with a 11 degree lens. The LED source is a Oslon 1 series in Warm White (3000k) with expected 111 lumens at 350mA.

Dimmer

The dimmer will be designed in a custom PCB for the project. The potentiometer attaches to the wooden frame (hole drilled by hand) and a wooden cap covers the back of the PCB outwards the lamp.

Dimmer assembly

Mirror Assembly

Mirror assembly installation

The mirror assembly is probably the trickiest part of the design. The idea is to have a mechanism to allow 2 degrees of movement to the mirror, meaning the light can be bounced around the lamp.
The convex mirror is a off-the-shelve item 4.8mm in diameter.
